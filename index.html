The Framework

Systematic evaluation of investment opportunities

Conviction-based investing requires three elements: deep research to understand the business, systematic evaluation to assess opportunities consistently, and transparent results to validate or refute the thesis.

This is not stock picking. This is building falsifiable hypotheses about mispriced businesses and testing them with real capital.

A

Cash Compounders

Overlooked businesses generating exceptional free cash flow that the market undervalues due to sector bias, boring narratives, or complexity.

FCF yield >10%
FCF conversion >60%
Sustainable competitive position
Trading at significant discount to growth sectors
B

Operational Inflections

Companies undergoing fundamental operational improvement that the market hasn't yet recognized or priced. The business today is materially different than what the market is valuing.

Sequential improvement in margins/revenue quality
Management executing on turnaround plan
Trading at trough multiples
Identifiable catalyst forcing recognition
C

Structural Advantages

Companies with durable competitive positions created by industry structure, not just company execution. Economics favor the incumbent.

High barriers to entry
Limited competition from industry dynamics
Pricing power from supply/demand imbalance
Secular tailwinds benefiting structural position
D

Temporary Impairment

High-quality businesses trading at distressed valuations due to temporary, fixable problems. Market confuses cyclical weakness with permanent impairment.

Historical ROIC >15%
Temporary headwind causing compression
Balance sheet can withstand downturn
Clear path to normalization (12-24 months)

Conviction Scoring System

Every thesis receives a 0-50 point score based on five dimensions

Cash Quality /10

FCF generation, conversion rates, balance sheet strength, capital efficiency

Growth Trajectory /10

Revenue/earnings momentum, margin expansion, forward visibility, runway

Competitive Position /10

Market structure, barriers to entry, pricing power, structural advantages

Entry Timing /10

Technical setup, sentiment positioning, catalyst proximity, risk/reward asymmetry

Valuation Gap /10

Discount to intrinsic value, peer multiples, margin of safety, rerating potential

40-50 Extreme Conviction 15-20% position
30-39 High Conviction 8-12% position
20-29 Moderate Conviction 3-7% position
<20 Low Conviction 0-3% position
